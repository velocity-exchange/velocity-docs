# P&L Pool

The **P&L Pool** is each perpetual market's pool of settled funds available for withdrawal — negative unrealised P&L that gets settled into it increases the amount available; positive P&L settled out of it decreases that amount. See [Accounting & Settlement](/protocol/trading/profit-loss/accounting-settlement) for how the pool is funded and prioritised against fee sweeps, and [Introduction to Profit & Loss](/protocol/trading/profit-loss) for how calling `settlePNL` relates to realising and claiming P&L.

<Callout type="important">
Calling `settlePNL` does not affect open positions — it only settles the funds available in the P&L Pool for withdrawal. It's important to recognise the difference between **settling P&L** and **realising P&L** — see [Introduction to Profit & Loss](/protocol/trading/profit-loss).
</Callout>

Any account can call `settlePNL` permissionlessly to settle all unrealised P&L for a position, adding to (or subtracting from) the market's P&L Pool.
