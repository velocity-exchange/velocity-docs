# Place Your First Trade

This guide walks you through the full loop of trading on Velocity for the first time: connecting a wallet, depositing collateral, opening a small perpetual futures position, keeping an eye on it, and closing it back out.

## Before You Start

Make sure you have:

- A Solana wallet (e.g. Phantom) with a small amount of SOL to cover network fees. See [Wallet Setup](/protocol/getting-started/wallet-setup) if you don't have one yet.
- Some funds to deposit as collateral — USDC is the simplest option, but SOL, BTC, ETH and other supported assets can also be used. See [Cross-Collateral Deposits](/protocol/getting-started/cross-collateral-deposits) for the full list of accepted assets.

<Callout type="warning">
Start small. Trading with leverage means your position can be liquidated (automatically closed out) if the market moves against you. Understand your liquidation price before opening a real position — see [Liquidations](/protocol/trading/liquidations) for how this works.
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Connect your wallet

Navigate to the trading page and select "Connect Wallet" in the top right corner. Choose your wallet (e.g. Phantom) and approve the connection request.

<img src="/assets/phantom-setup-1.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

<img src="/assets/phantom-setup-2.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

### Deposit collateral

Click the "Deposit" button next to your connected wallet address, choose the asset and amount you want to deposit, then confirm and sign the transaction in your wallet.

<img src="/assets/phantom-setup-3.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

<img src="/assets/phantom-setup-4.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

This deposit becomes your **margin** — the collateral backing your positions and determining how much you can trade. Read more about how margin works on [Margin](/protocol/trading/margin).

### Choose a market and direction

Select the market you want to trade from the top navigation bar (e.g. `Trade` > `Perpetuals`) and pick your market. Then choose Long (Buy) if you expect the price to rise, or Short (Sell) if you expect it to fall.

<img src="/assets/perpetuals-trading-1.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

<Callout type="important">
For your first trade, keep it simple and use a Market Order — this fills immediately at the best available price rather than waiting for a specific price. Velocity also offers Limit Orders and other order types for more control; see [Order Types](/protocol/trading/order-types) once you're ready to explore them.
</Callout>

### Set your position size

Enter how much you want to trade, either as the amount of the underlying asset, the amount of USDC collateral to use, or by dragging the leverage slider. **Leverage** lets you open a larger position than your collateral alone would allow — for example, 2x leverage means a $100 deposit controls a $200 position — but it also scales your gains and losses in the same proportion, and increases your liquidation risk. For your first trade, keep leverage low (or 1x).

You can also set your maximum allowed **slippage** — the amount of price movement you're willing to tolerate between placing the order and it filling.

<img src="/assets/perpetuals-trading-2.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

### Review and confirm

Click the button at the bottom of the trade form to open the position. A confirmation modal will show you the details of your trade, including your estimated **liquidation price** — the price at which your position would be automatically closed to prevent further losses. Review it carefully, then confirm.

<img src="/assets/perpetuals-trading-3.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

Behind the scenes, your market order is routed through a brief (~5 second) Just-In-Time auction where market makers compete to fill it at the best price, falling back to Velocity's AMM if no maker fills it. You don't need to do anything during this — it happens automatically. See [How It Works](/protocol/how-it-works) if you'd like to understand this in more depth.

### Monitor your position

Once filled, your position appears under the "Positions" tab, where you can track your entry price, current P&L, and liquidation price at any time. If the market moves against you and gets close to your liquidation price, consider adding more collateral or reducing your position size.

### Close your position

When you're ready to exit, go to the "Positions" tab and click "Market / Limit" next to your position to realize your P&L, or "Close All" to close every open position at once. Confirm the order type and size in the modal that appears.

<img src="/assets/perpetuals-trading-4.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

<img src="/assets/perpetuals-trading-5.png" alt="" width="800" height="auto"></img>

Once closed, your Balances and Total Collateral will update to reflect the realized P&L from the trade.

</Steps>
